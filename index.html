⭐ WHAT THIS TOOL DOES

Takes your PM schedule, analyzes monthly workload by trade (Engineers, HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical, General Mechanic), identifies overloaded months, and intelligently reschedules PMs to balance the workload — while protecting critical equipment and respecting seasonal windows.

🚀 QUICK-START (5 minutes)
Step 1 — Select Hospital
Use the dropdown in the left sidebar. Each hospital has its own isolated data.
Step 2 — Upload Your PM Schedule
Below these instructions, click 📅 PM Schedule → upload your .xlsx file. Required columns: PM Name, Building, Frequency Desc, Next Scheduled Date. Dates should be current (2025 or later) — PMs older than 3 months show as past-due and don't appear on the calendar grid.
Step 3 — Set Manpower Capacity
Go to 👷 Manpower Capacity in the sidebar. Default values are set for MWHC (2 Eng, 4 HVAC, 2 Plumb, 3 Elec, 7 GM mechanics). Edit the counts to match your actual crew.
Step 4 — Click ▶ Run Load Leveling
Top of page, blue/gold button. The engine will analyze your schedule and reschedule PMs to balance workload.
Step 5 — Review Results
🏠 Dashboard shows moves, peak reduction, critical protection. Click any metric card for drill-down.
📆 Calendar View shows before-vs-after dates. Click any date to see PMs due.
📊 LL Summary Report shows the LL Score (A-F grade), executive summary, and has 2 download buttons.
Step 6 — Adjust if needed
On Manpower Capacity, drag the 🎚️ Threshold Override slider to force more/fewer moves. Changes apply instantly — no need to click Run again. Or switch strategy: Conservative / Moderate / Aggressive on the Balanced Schedule page.
Step 7 — Export
On 📊 LL Summary Report:
• 📊 Download Adjusted Schedule (xlsx) — 5-sheet Excel for pushing back to Maintenance Connection
• 📋 Internal Detail Report (PDF) — printable report with every PM listed, for internal team use
• 🏥 MedStar Ready PDF — 10-slide executive deck in MedStar/Crothall branding, for leadership presentations
🎚️ THE SLIDER — WHAT IT DOES
The threshold slider (on Manpower Capacity page) controls how strictly the engine treats capacity:
🔴 50% Max Force — flag overloads when trades hit just half capacity. Forces aggressive rebalancing.
🟠 70% Force Moves — keeps 30% headroom; forces most PMs to balance.
85% Tight — keeps 15% headroom for service calls.
🔵 100% Default — uses your actual stated capacity as the threshold.
125-150% Relax — allows overbooking. Only fixes severe overloads.
⚠️ IMPORTANT NOTES
  • Data is browser-local. Your uploaded data stays in your browser. A partner on a different computer won't see your uploads — they upload their own.
  • Critical PMs are protected. Mark critical equipment in the PM modal (click any PM) — the engine will never move them.
  • Seasonal rules apply automatically. Cooling tower PMs stay Apr–Oct, heating equipment stays Nov–Mar.
  • Cascade projection. When a recurring PM moves, future instances (quarterly, annual, etc.) auto-project the same shift — visible in the drill-down and xlsx export.
  • Stale banner. If you change config after running LL, an orange banner appears on the Dashboard prompting a re-run.
🆘 TROUBLESHOOTING
"No moves are happening" — your workload fits within capacity at default threshold. Drag slider to 70% or lower to force moves.
"Calendar is empty" — check your PM dates. If all are in the past (e.g. 2021), they show as past-due. Update the xlsx with current dates.
"PDF report is blank" — make sure Load Leveling has been run. The report uses the LL output.
"Popup blocked" — PDF opens in a new window. Allow popups for this site, or use the HTML fallback download.

2. Reading the Numbers
Every metric you'll see explained with examples.
277d
Total Days Shifted
What it means: Total days every PM moved, added together (always positive — both pulls and pushes count).

Example: PM #1 moved 61d, PM #2 moved 30d, PM #3 moved 15d → 106d total.

How to read it: Lower is less disruption. ~3 days × number of moved PMs is mild. 5+ days each = significant rework.
6.2d
Avg Shift
What it means: Total days shifted ÷ number of moved PMs.

How to read it: The typical move size. Under 7 days = most PMs barely budged. 30+ days = many PMs moved a full month.
62d
Max Shift
What it means: The largest single PM move.

How to read it: Worst-case displacement. Capped by the strategy: Conservative ≤30d, Moderate ≤62d, Aggressive ≤90d.
59%
Peak Reduction
What it means: How much the busiest month dropped after leveling.

Example: May had 22 PMs, after = 9 PMs → (22-9)/22 = 59% reduction.

How to read it: Higher = better. 50%+ is a strong outcome.
45
PMs Moved
What it means: Number of PMs the engine rescheduled.

How to read it: Usually 30–50% of total PMs. The rest stayed on their original date because they were already in a good month.
94.2%
Volatility Reduction
What it means: Statistical smoothness gain. Measures how flat the monthly workload is now vs. before.

How to read it: 90%+ = essentially flat. 50–80% = noticeably smoother. Under 30% = engine couldn't move much.
8 → 0
Overloads
What it means: Number of trade-month cells that exceeded crew capacity, before vs. after. Each cell = one trade in one month.

How to read it: Goal is After = 0. If it's not zero, the slider can be pushed lower.
12
Critical Locked
What it means: PMs flagged Critical (chillers, boilers, generators, life safety) that the engine refused to move.

Why: Critical assets are protected from rescheduling. They keep their original date no matter how busy that month gets.

4. Threshold Slider
On the Manpower Capacity page. Drag to force more or fewer moves. Updates instantly — no need to click Run.
50% Force 100% Default 150% Relax
⬅ Drag LEFT (50–90%)
Tightens the threshold — engine flags more overloads — forces more moves. Use when you want a flatter schedule.
100% (Default)
Uses your real configured manpower. Engine moves PMs only when a trade is genuinely overloaded.
DRAG RIGHT (110–150%) ➡
Relaxes — engine tolerates more overload before moving. Fewer PMs get rescheduled.
💡 Tip: If your manpower config is way bigger than your workload, the slider auto-fits to your peak month so it's always responsive — you'll see PMs move at every position.
5. Strategy Buttons
On the Balanced Schedule page. Controls how aggressively the engine reschedules.
🛡️ Conservative
PMs can move ±1 month max. Capacity treated as 100% real. Fewer moves, smaller shifts. Use when CMMS dates are mostly correct already.
⚖️ Moderate (default)
PMs can move ±2 months. Capacity treated as 95%. Balanced — the engine handles most overloads without dragging things across seasons.
⚡ Aggressive
PMs can move ±3 months. Capacity treated as 85%. Maximum smoothing. Use when the schedule is wildly stacked and you need to flatten it hard.
6. Season Mode Toggle 🔀
Decides WHEN cooling and heating PMs are scheduled. Toggle is on the Seasonal Planning page. The badge in the top bar always shows which mode is active.
✅ IN-SEASON Mode (default)
🟠 Cooling: Apr–Oct🔵 Heating: Nov–Mar
Service while running. You can observe live operation — catch noise, vibration, leaks, refrigerant issues. Best for inspections, oil analysis, vibration checks.
🔄 OFF-SEASON Mode (flipped)
🟠 Cooling: Nov–Mar🔵 Heating: Apr–Oct
Service while idle. Equipment is offline anyway, so teardowns and overhauls don't risk patient comfort. Best for boiler tube cleaning, eddy current testing, deaerator service.
💡 Tip: When you toggle modes, all reports auto-update — calendar shows new dates, slider re-runs, PDFs reflect the choice. Move reasons explain the mode in plain English ("⬆ Pulled forward 30d. Strategy: moderate, Season mode: OFF-SEASON (flipped)").

10. Glossary
Load Leveling (LL)The process of redistributing PM dates to flatten monthly workload while respecting capacity, season, and criticality.
Pulled forwardPM moved to an EARLIER date than originally scheduled (the green ⬆ arrow). Used when the original month is overloaded.
Pushed outPM moved to a LATER date (the orange ⬇ arrow). Used when an earlier month has more spare capacity.
Critical PMPM on chillers, boilers, generators, transfer switches, deaerators, or any asset flagged Critical. These are LOCKED — engine never moves them.
CascadeFuture occurrences of a recurring PM. If you move a quarterly PM by 30 days, all four future quarters cascade by 30 days too.
Stale bannerThe yellow warning at the top of the Dashboard that means: "your settings changed but you haven't re-run LL yet — the numbers below may be out of date."
Trade capacityPMs/month a single trade can handle = (mechanics) × (workdays/month) × (% time available for PMs).
Auto-fit modeWhen configured manpower is way too high for your workload (under 30% utilization), the engine auto-pegs capacity to your actual peak so the slider stays responsive.
